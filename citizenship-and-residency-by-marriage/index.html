The 4-Step Ladder to Citizenship

According to the Ministry of Foreign Affairs (Resolution 5477) and Nationality Law, you must respect this strict hierarchy. One administrative mistake resets your clock to zero.

Step 1

The Legal Union

Before applying, you must formalize your relationship in Colombia. You have two paths:

  • Civil Marriage: Grants immediate visa eligibility.
  • Common-Law Union: You must wait more than 1 year of legally established union before applying.
Step 2

The M-Type Visa

This is your "Spouse Visa". It is your entry ticket and allows you to live and work. The Trap: If you leave Colombia for more than 180 consecutive days, your visa is canceled and your residency clock resets.

Step 3

R-Type Residency

This is where your union type changes everything:

  • Civil Marriage: Apply after holding the M-Visa for 3 years.
  • Common-Law Union: Apply after holding the M-Visa for 5 years.
Step 4

Citizenship

Only after you hold your R-Type Resident Visa, you establish legal domicile. As the spouse of a Colombian citizen, your mandatory waiting time is reduced to 2 years before you can take the nationality tests.

Expectation vs. Reality

Most foreigners make mistakes in Year 1 that cost them their residency in Year 3. Where do you currently stand?

✗
"We just declared our Common-Law Union yesterday, let's apply for the visa."

Reality: Denied. The law strictly requires the Common-Law Union to be legally formalized for more than 1 year before you become eligible for the Spouse Visa.

✗
"We got married in the US/Europe, so I can apply right away with that certificate."

Reality: Denied. You cannot apply until your foreign marriage is officially registered before a Colombian Notary or Consulate, which requires apostilles and official translations.

✗
"I have my Spouse M-Visa, I can travel back to my home country for 8 months to work."

Reality: Visa Canceled. Leaving for more than 180 consecutive days automatically kills your visa. You lose all your accumulated progress toward Residency.
